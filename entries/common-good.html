Personhood and the Common Good

"Me" vs. "We"

It's easy to get caught up in our own goals, our own problems, and our own success. Modern culture often promotes a very individualistic mindset. But this topic made me question that: how much of my well-being is tied to the well-being of the community around me?

The Group Project Analogy

I thought about a recent group project in one of my classes. Initially, I just wanted to do my part quickly and get it over with. But one member of our group went the extra mile. They organized our meetings, created a shared document, and helped anyone who was struggling. Their individual effort didn't just improve their own work; it elevated the entire project and reduced everyone's stress.

We all received a better grade because of their dedication to the "common good" of our small group. It was a perfect example of how valuing the success of the whole doesn't diminish the individual; it enhances them. My contribution became more meaningful because it was part of a successful collective effort. It taught me that we truly do grow together.
